About me

Short Bio

Welcome to my homepage! I am Kai Sun (孙锴), a senior undergraduate at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, studying Computer Science in ACM Honored Class 2011. I worked with Prof. Alei Liang on swarm intelligence at SJTU Embedded Lab from Feb. 2012 to Oct. 2012. After that, I have been working with Prof. Kai Yu on speech at SJTU Speech Lab since Jun. 2013. In Jul. 2014, I went to Cornell University for a one-month summer program headed by Prof. John E. Hopcroft . I started working with principal researcher Frank Soong on speech for a six-month internship at Microsoft Research Asia (MSRA) from Aug. 2014.

Areas of Special Interests

Dialogue Management, Machine Learning, and AI in Games, currently focusing on dialogue state tracking.

Selected Research Experience

  • Research on Assessment of the Academic Impact of China, supervised by John E. Hopcroft Dec. 2012 - Jan. 2013

    Our team would like to design a system to create a list of top research faculty at US universities who are Asian, and ranks each name by quality of research, area of China originally from. This research involves some project work like extracting names from a web page and some AI research to create a program to tell if a name is Asian, American, or Muslim.

    Report

  • Research on AI in Board Games 2006 - Present

    I designed my first Chinese chess AI program in my junior middle school. Since then, I have been interested in AI in board games and researching on it in my spare time. I have read a large number of materials and independently designed several AI programs including Chinese chess, go, Gomoku/Renju, etc.. Of the various board games, Gomoku/Renju is my favourite one. Yixin, a state-of-the-art Gomoku/Renju program designed by me, is the winner of the 13th, the 14th and the 15th Gomocup, significantly outperforming the AI in the second position with a gap of about 140 elo, and is currently the strongest Gomoku/Renju program in the world.

    More about Yixin & Gomocup

  • Research on Dialogue Management, supervised by Kai Yu Jun. 2013 - Jun. 2014

    We researched on improving the state of the art in tracking the state of spoken dialogue systems using both rule-based and statistical approaches. We built several dialogue state tracking systems, and investigated and refined the theoretical base of rule-based approaches. Besides, we proposed a novel framework, referred to as Markov Bayesian Polynomial (MBP) for dialogue state tracking.

    Our team participated in the 2nd Dialog State Tracking Challenge (DSTC2) and ranked the 3rd. Our team then participated in the 3rd Dialog State Tracking Challenge (DSTC3) and our method was found to be one of the top three tracking methods, and was one of the only two methods that outperformed all four baselines of the challenge.

    More about DSTC2 & DSTC3

  • Research on Speech Synthesis, supervised by Frank Soong and Lijuan Wang Aug. 2014 - Present

    I researched on Recurrent Neural Network (RNN)-based text-to-speech (TTS) sysnthesis sytem and RNN-based 3D talking head.

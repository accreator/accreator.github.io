  • Research on AI in Board Games 2006 - Now

    I designed my first Chinese chess AI program in my junior middle school. Since then, I have been interested in AI in board games and researching on it in my spare time. I have read a large number of materials and independently designed several AI programs including Chinese chess, go, Gomoku/Renju, etc.. Of the various board games, Gomoku/Renju is my favourite one. Yixin, a state-of-the-art Gomoku/Renju program designed by me, is the winner of the 13th, the 14th and the 15th Gomocup, significantly outperforming the AI in the second position with a gap of about 140 elo, and is currently the strongest Gomoku/Renju program in the world.

  • Research on Dialogue Management, supervised by Kai Yu Jun.2013 - Now

    We research on improving the state of the art in tracking the state of spoken dialogue systems using both rule-based and statistical approaches. For the statistical approach, we have implemented maximum entropy, extreme learning machine, support vector machine, deep neural network, and recurrent neural network with rich features. For the rule-based approach, we investigate and refine the theoretical base of rule-based models. Our team participated in the 2nd Dialog State Tracking Challenge (DSTC2) and ranked the 3rd. Our team then participated in the 3rd Dialog State Tracking Challenge (DSTC3) and our method was found to be one of the top three tracking methods, and was one of the only two methods that outperform all four baselines of the challenge.
